The development and use of AI raises important ethical questions, such as issues of bias and privacy
that must be carefully considered.

  • Bias: AI algorithms can perpetuate and amplify existing biases in society, such as racial and gender biases, if not properly designed and tested.
  • Privacy: AI systems often collect and process large amounts of personal data, which can raise concerns about privacy and data protection.
  • Transparency: AI systems can be opaque, making it difficult for users to understand how decisions are being made and to identify potential biases.
  • Weaponization: The use of AI in military and defense applications raises ethical questions about the development and use of autonomous weapons.

  • AI is also not neutral: AI-based decisions are susceptible to inaccuracies, discriminatory outcomes, embedded or inserted bias.
  • AI decisions are not always intelligible to humans.
