Automated decision making (ADM) systems, which use algorithms and machine learning to make decisions,
can raise a number of ethical issues, including:

  • Bias: ADM systems can perpetuate and amplify existing biases in society, such as racial and gender biases, if not properly designed and tested.
  • Privacy: ADM systems often collect and process large amounts of personal data, which can raise concerns about privacy and data protection.
  • Fairness: ADM systems can make decisions that have a significant impact on people's lives, such as in hiring or lending decisions, and there are ethical questions around ensuring that these
    decisions are fair and unbiased.
  • Autonomy: As ADM systems become more advanced, they may be able to make decisions and take actions without human intervention, raising questions about who is responsible for their actions.

Overall, it is important to ensure that ADM systems are designed and implemented in a way that is ethical and transparent,
and that they are subject to appropriate oversight and accountability mechanisms.
